JSRender

JSRender is my attempt at a custom built render-engine. It's built on top of the HTML5 Canvas and it renders a 3D textured cube on the screen.

View the Project on GitHub

The basics

This is a rather small demo I created of a simplified solar system, consisting of the Sun and eight surrounding planets.

View the live example here

What really pleased me is that Three.js managed all the scene complexity for me: adding and assigning VBOs, IBOs, buffers, etc, was hidden behind an elegant system of scene, camera, geometry and material management.

Which fitted me perfectly, because for I long time I wanted to explore shaders in depth, but without the hassle of setting up the traditional OpenGL or WebGL environment.

Shaders

For this example I created two custom shader pairs (vertex and fragment), besides the one thas come with Three.js.

The first vertex shader is a very simple one that does the basic gl_Position calculation and then passes that info the fragment shader. This in turn calculates a very simple point light (very little ambient color, diffuse with no attenuation, and no specilar component ) to give the planets a shadow / dark side when not facing the sun.

The second pair of shaders act as a post-processing step of sorts, performing a horizontal and then vertical blur on the scene, to give the planets and sun a "blurry edge".
